Win Conditions

  • Capture the enemy King
  • Move your King to the Castle
  • Occupy the enemy fort with no defenders
HOW TO WIN

1. Capture the opponent's King.

2. Move your King onto the Castle (centre square) and survive until it is your turn again.

3. Have at least one of your pieces in the opponent's fort while they have no pieces in their own fort.

MOVEMENT

Most pieces slide in a straight line and cannot jump over another piece.

The Axe and Hunter make a knight's leap. Legal only if a straight line from their square to the target doesn't cross an occupied square — pieces diagonally corner-to-corner also block.

Shieldwall: a one-step diagonal move is blocked when both orthogonally adjacent squares are occupied.

Capture by landing on an enemy piece.

SKJOLDING (V)

Advances two squares forward (square ahead must be clear), captures one square diagonally forward, steps one square back. Diagonally placed Skjoldings guard each other.

OFFICERS
KKing — one step any direction. Reaches Castle to win.
SpSpearman — wide reach ahead; narrow retreat.
BwBowman — slides up to 4 forward; one step sideways.
BkBerserker — three forward lanes up to 3; one step sideways.
SwSword — one step orthogonally, or diagonal slide up to 4.
WoWolf — orthogonal slide up to 3.
AxAxe — short ortho/diagonal step, plus knight's leap.
HuHunter — diagonal step, plus knight's leap.
STRATEGY

Hold the centre with Spearman, Bowman, Berserker to block the enemy King.

Develop Wolf and Hunter to the flanks first, Sword and Axe behind them.

The King's march to the Castle is a winning threat — guard your own Fort too.

Material edge? Press both flanks at once — one must give.
